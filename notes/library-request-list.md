## Highest priority

### Fahnestock 1999

Jeanne Fahnestock. 1999. *Rhetorical Figures in Science*. New York: Oxford University Press.

- DOI: `10.1093/oso/9780195117509.001.0001`
- ISBN: `9780195117509`
- Need: full book or ebook access.
- Status: acquired as five Oxford chapter/part PDFs in `fahnestock1999-rhetorical-figures-in-science/`.
- Why: central theoretical anchor for the claim that figures are form-function resources in scientific argument, not ornamental style.

### Fahnestock 2011

Jeanne Fahnestock. 2011. *Rhetorical Style: The Uses of Language in Persuasion*. Oxford University Press.

- DOI: `10.1093/acprof:oso/9780199764129.001.0001`
- ISBN: `9780199764129`
- Need: full book or ebook access.
- Status: acquired as introduction plus ten chapter PDFs in `fahnestock2011-rhetorical-style/`.
- Why: broader and more accessible formulation of rhetorical figures as persuasive resources.

### Fahnestock 2004, "Preserving the Figure"

Jeanne Fahnestock. 2004. "Preserving the Figure: Consistency in the Presentation of Scientific Arguments." *Written Communication* 21(1): 6-31.

- DOI: `10.1177/0741088303261034`
- Need: article PDF.
- Status: acquired as `fahnestock_2004_preserving_the_figure.pdf`.
- Why: directly relevant to stability of figural presentation across argument contexts.

### RST Signalling Corpus data

Debopam Das, Maite Taboada, and Paul McFetridge. 2015. *RST Signalling Corpus LDC2015T10*. Linguistic Data Consortium.

- DOI: `10.35111/5sm9-m096`
- LDC catalog: `https://catalog.ldc.upenn.edu/LDC2015T10`
- Need: institutional access or licensed download.
- Status: pending until library/LDC access is available.
- Why: best comparison corpus for relation signals outside eRST/GUM, especially if we want WSJ-based comparability with earlier discourse parsing work.
