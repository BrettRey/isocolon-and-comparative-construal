# Rhetoricon Note

Date: 2026-05-29

Event: Rhetoricon, University of Waterloo.

Trigger: Gini Fahnestock proposed that isocolon has the effect of making comparison more salient, pointed, explicit, or forceful. Brett asked after the talk whether anyone had tried quantitative work to test the idea. It seemed not.

Resulting project idea: test a smaller corpus-distributional claim first. Instead of asking whether isocolon changes reader perception, ask whether isocolon is more common in clauses or clause pairs making comparisons than in clauses or clause pairs with other discourse relations.

Current methodological position: the corpus study is probative if framed as production-side textual evidence and if comparison pairs are matched against other binary relations with similar syntactic opportunities for parallelism.
