# Non-Obvious Review Board

This board is for stress-testing the paper from strong but not predictable angles. It deliberately avoids the most obvious local choices: Jeanne Fahnestock, Randy Harris, Amir Zeldes, Maite Taboada, Debopam Das, and direct custodians of the resources under analysis. Those people are useful interlocutors, but they are not the best simulated reviewer board for finding blind spots.

## Core Six

### Ted Sanders

Discourse studies, coherence relations, and discourse processing. He should press whether comparable-set construal is a real discourse-level generalization or a soft label for coordination and enumeration.

Primary review question: Does the paper keep corpus-distributional claims distinct from claims about reader processing?

### Manfred Stede

RST, discourse parsing, coherence relations, and corpus-based discourse resources. He should evaluate the use of eRST labels, adjacency assumptions, relation extraction, graph/concurrent-relation handling, and the interpretation of label mismatch.

Primary review question: Are the discourse-relation units and labels being used in a way a discourse-annotation expert would accept?

### Rashmi Prasad

Discourse-relation annotation, PDTB, shallow discourse annotation, and cross-framework relation inventories. She should press on relation taxonomy, explicit versus implicit relations, annotation reliability, and whether the paper asks eRST labels to do too much.

Primary review question: Are the coding categories and validation claims strong enough for a relation-annotation audience?

### Stefan Th. Gries

Corpus linguistics, quantitative methods, and regression modelling. He should be the hostile methods reviewer: effect sizes, collinearity, model specification, document clustering, robustness checks, hand-set weights, and interpretation of small but statistically stable effects.

Primary review question: Are the estimated effects interpretable as corpus evidence rather than artifacts of the scoring and controls?

### Gerard Steen

Figurative-language identification, metaphor-in-discourse methods, and annotation protocols such as MIPVU. He should assess the form/function distinction: a surface candidate is not automatically a functioning figure, and a figure label needs an operational coding rule.

Primary review question: Does the audit justify claims about rhetorical function rather than merely formal resemblance?

### Daniel Altshuler

Coordination and the syntax--discourse interface. He should be the coordination skeptic: lists and disjunctions may be parallel because coordination makes matched categories cheap, not because writers are deploying rhetorical balance.

Primary review question: Does any signal survive once grammar-driven coordination is treated as the null hypothesis?

## Alternates

### Vera Demberg

Computational and experimental discourse processing. Use her if the paper needs a stronger reader-response/discourse-processing check, especially around what corpus evidence can and cannot say about construal.

### Bonnie Webber

Formal and computational discourse relations, PDTB, discourse connectives, and implicit relation inference. Use her if the board needs a second high-level discourse-relation formalist instead of a figurative-language reviewer.

### Elena Semino

Corpus stylistics and figurative-language analysis. Use her if the paper needs a more literary-stylistic reviewer who will care about examples, rhetorical texture, and the qualitative audit.
